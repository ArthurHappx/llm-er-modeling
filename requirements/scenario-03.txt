ER Modeling Scenario
Integrated Hospital Management System for Multiple Facilities
Business Context
You have been hired to model the database for a hospital network that operates 15 units across 3 Brazilian states. The system must control, record and monitor the entry and exit of all people who access the hospitals, including staff, visitors and patients, ensuring traceability, security and compliance with the institution's internal rules.
Functional Requirements
1. Hospital
Each hospital has departments responsible for specific activities. Not every facility in the network needs to offer all services: each hospital declares the departments, human resources and capacities it has (available teams and departments).
Departments present in the network:
* Emergency
* ICU (Intensive Care Unit)
* Surgical Center / Operating Theaters
* Adult Ward
* Pediatric Ward
* Maternity
* Radiology / Imaging (X-ray, CT, MRI)
* Clinical Laboratory
* Outpatient Clinic
* Hospital Pharmacy
* Social Services
* Triage
* Reception
1.1 Departments
* Each department has an assigned coordinator.
* There must be a leader or person responsible for the department in every shift (there should always be someone accountable for the department at any time).

2. People Management
The hospital receives individuals in multiple categories; some are employees and others are not. Categories include: visitor, patient, physician, nurse, resident physician, researcher and administrative staff. Physicians may work in multiple units under different contract types (for example, CLT, PJ, on-call, daily-rate). Residents are licensed physicians in training who also attend patients under supervision, so it is important to record the supervising physician for each resident. VIP patients have special care rules and enhanced privacy (these are procedural and privacy measures, not related to the medical quality of care). Employees may have dependents who are also patients. The hospital records the following for every person who accesses it: full name, addresses, contact phone numbers, CPF, RG and date of birth. (CPF and RG are Brazilian identifiers: CPF = taxpayer ID, RG = identity card.)
2.1 Patients
   * Each patient receives an identification wristband.
   * The system must record patient movements between departments, including hospital admission, transfers between departments and discharge.
   * A complete history of the departments each patient has passed through must be maintained.
   * The system must indicate whether the patient is awaiting care, in care, admitted (inpatient), under observation, in surgery, or discharged.
2.2 Visitors
   * Not every visitor is accompanying a patient.
   * The type of visitor must be recorded (for example, technical visit, regulatory inspection, etc.).
   * Visitor classification is essential to determine access restrictions (important information for the security team).
   * Each visitor receives an identification card at entry.
   * Not every patient is allowed to have a companion. Valid companion cases include: minors; elderly patients; pregnant patients; women in labor; postpartum patients; patients with prolonged or severe hospitalization, at the discretion of the attending physician or care team; and situations of emotional or cognitive vulnerability when the physician or social services deem it necessary.
   * If patient isolation is required, the physician must document the reason for isolation in a clinical note.
   * The system must record the visitor's exit (mandatory).
   * Access outside visiting hours must be prevented unless special authorization is granted by the hospital.
2.3 Employees
   * Employees must be clearly identified.
   * A researcher can be either a nurse or a physician only; researcher is not an independent occupational category.
   * A resident physician cannot be a coordinator or be responsible for a department, even temporarily.
   * Physicians and nurses may also hold administrative roles (for example, responsible for coordinating a department).
   * Any employee may still be treated by the hospital, be present as a visitor, or accompany a patient.
2.4 Addresses
   * A person may have more than one registered address.
   * Employees update their addresses periodically.
   * When possible, patients and visitors should also have updated addresses.
   * It is important to store the timestamp of the last address update.
   * If the last update was more than six months ago, the system should alert and request a registration update.
2.5 Identification Cards
   * Identification cards have an issue date and an expiry date, recorded in the system for security purposes.
   * Cards have a type that designates access level (visually distinguished by different colors).
   * When a card is in use it is marked as active.
   * Because patients and visitors do not have personalized cards, a card's identifier number may be temporarily linked to the person's record while in use.
   * Only personalized cards (employees) must include a photo.
3. Employee Scheduling
Hospital units require an integrated time and attendance system that covers all 15 units to prevent staff from being accidentally scheduled at two locations at the same time or assigned impractical schedules. Professionals follow different work routines. Administration must precisely control the hours worked by each employee. Different hospitals in the network may have different scheduling contracts with the same employee. It is also necessary to record events that remove an employee from duties, such as days off, medical leave, vacation or training.
3.1 Time Clock Records
   * The system must record each employee's clock-in and clock-out times.
   * As mentioned, each employee has an identification card, which facilitates this task.
   * Time records may generate an alert if an employee exceeds scheduled hours or misses a clock record; this is useful for HR.
3.2 Work Shifts
   * Some employees have more flexible shifts than others.
   * Administrative staff generally have fixed weekdays and start/end times.
   * Nurses may have fixed rules or work rotating shifts.
   * Physicians similarly may have variable schedules and may come to the hospital only to perform consultations, monitoring, surgeries, etc., leaving when those activities are completed.
   * The employment contract governs how working hours are administered by the contracting institution.
   * The key requirement is accurate recording of clock-in and clock-out times for HR verification of compliance with agreed schedules.
3.3 Availability Exceptions
   * Reasons that remove a professional from scheduled activities must be well documented.
   * Each exception should include start and end dates, type (day off, medical leave, vacation, training) and an optional textual note.
3.4 Shift Swaps
   * Shift swaps among health staff are common and permitted.
   * Whenever a swap occurs, it is important to record the involved employees and the coordinator who approved the swap.
   * Each department has its coordinator, who may be a physician, a nurse or an administrative employee.
   * The justification for the swap request should also be recorded (useful for HR metrics about the need for permanent schedule changes, employee health considerations, etc.).
4. Special Situations and Contingency Procedures
4.1 Lost Identification Card
The system must allow immediate registration of a lost card. When a loss is reported:
   * the card is automatically invalidated;
   * a new card may be issued and linked to the person;
   * every access attempt using the lost card is blocked and logged;
   * the event generates an alert for security.
A history of cards (active, replaced, lost, expired) must be maintained.
4.2 Card Failure (Defective or Inoperable Card)
When a card fails to read, the system must record: the person involved; the reason for the failure; the read attempt; and the manual verification performed (security or reception staff responsible). Manual validation of entry/exit using an official document plus employee ID/registration must be allowed. After manual validation, the system records the entry/exit with the same legal validity as an electronic read. The defective card should be marked inoperable and require replacement.
5. Reports and Queries
      * Query access history by person, date, department and type (employee, visitor, patient).
      * Generate department occupancy reports.
      * Generate reports on compliance with scheduled hours and total hours worked for a given period (month, week, day).
      * The system must alert whenever a department reaches 60%, 80% and 100% of its operational capacity.
